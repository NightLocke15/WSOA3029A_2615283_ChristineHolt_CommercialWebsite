Fruit Sugar Content what this means:

Like most food, all fruits contain sugar. Differening levels of sugar, but they contain sugar. However, th statement that the sugars contained in fruit can be harmful to a person is a myth (Gunnars, 2018) (unless you eat an inhumane amount, but please do not do that). Fruits are healthy, this remains a fact, and it should be something that is incorporated into your daily diet to help you stay healthy (Jones, 2019). However, there might be several reasons why one would rather eat lower sugar content fruit. Some diets, like keto, works better when eating fruits with lower sugar content (Gunnars, 2018), and high sugar content fruit is something someone living with diabetes might want to avoid.

To aid in the quest for finding fruit with lower sugar content, showcased below is an interactive visualisation that separates 49 fruit into lowest, relatively low, relatively high and highest. The NHS (2022) states that when trying to eat less sugar, it is ideal to keep sugar intake to 5 grams per 100 grams of food. However, when eating whole fruits, the body neds to break it down, meaning the sugar does not hit the liver all at once. This makes the sugar in fruits basically harmless, though its is important for people with diabetes to keep track of the fruits they eat (Gunnars, 2018). Whether you eat fruit with higher or lower sugar content, it is still significantly more healthy than a lot of the common snacks and drinks manufactured today. A good example is a specific well known soda, which is notorious for the fact that it has high sugar levels. So, even when eating fruits with higher sugar contents, it is still healthier than most food with added sugar.

In conclusion, eat fruit. It is healthy, not only because the sugar contents are often lower, but also because the contain healthy vitamins and minerals, that are important for your health. They make a great snack, as they often satiate the craving for something sweet without being unhealthy (Gunnars, 2018). But if you want to limit the sugar intake from fruits for whatever reason, you still have a myriad of delicious fruit to choose from, all with their own great taste and health benefits. It is always good to eat things in recommended portions, as too much of anything is not a good thing, but if you are craving something sweet, reach for one of the many fruits available to you. It tastes good, it is good for you, and more often than not, it is good for your wallet.
